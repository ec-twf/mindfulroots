A focus supplement is a nutrient or plant extract taken to support normal attention and mental clarity. It is not a cognitive drug, and not a fix for an attention disorder. The framing here is narrow: the evidence supports a modest edge on top of the real drivers of focus, which are sleep, movement, and managing distraction. If concentration problems are significant or long-standing, that is worth a professional conversation rather than a supplement. Here is what each option actually supports.

## L-theanine: best with caffeine

L-theanine, an amino acid from tea, promotes a calm-but-alert state, and its strongest evidence for attention is not on its own but *paired with caffeine*. The combination supports focus while smoothing caffeine's jitter and crash, which is why it is the one focus stack with genuine human trials behind it. Evidence tier: **Moderate**. The [L-theanine pick](/products/l-theanine/) uses a standardized form.

## Rhodiola: for focus degraded by stress

Rhodiola rosea is studied for the mental fatigue that comes with stress, and that is where it may help focus, when your concentration is being eroded by being tired and under pressure rather than by distraction alone. Evidence tier: **Moderate**, for stress-related mental fatigue specifically. Our [ashwagandha vs rhodiola comparison](/blog/adaptogens-ashwagandha-rhodiola/) covers where it fits; the [rhodiola pick](/products/rhodiola-rosea/) is standardized.

## B-complex: only if you are short

B vitamins support brain energy metabolism, so a genuine deficiency can show up as poor concentration. Correcting it helps; supplementing on top of adequate levels does not sharpen a healthy baseline. Evidence tier: **Moderate**, conditional on your status. The [B-complex pick](/products/b-complex/) uses active forms, and our [energy and fatigue guide](/guides/energy/) covers B vitamins in more depth.

## How to choose

For most people the evidence points one way: **L-theanine with caffeine** is the focus pairing worth trying first. If your focus problems ride on stress and fatigue, **rhodiola** targets that pattern. **B-complex** only earns a place if you have reason to think you are low. None of these outranks the boring fundamentals. A full night's sleep does more for focus than any supplement here, which is worth remembering before you spend anything.

Add one supplement at a time so you can tell what helps, and talk to a doctor or pharmacist first, especially if you take other medication or are sensitive to caffeine. This page is general information, not medical advice; please read our [disclaimer](/disclaimer/).
