A sleep supplement is a nutrient or amino acid taken to support the body's normal wind-down, not a sedative, and not a treatment for insomnia. The point is exactly that: the two supplements with reasonable human evidence here help you relax toward sleep, they do not switch you off. If your sleep problems are persistent, they are worth a conversation with a clinician rather than a supplement aisle. Here is what each option actually supports.

## Magnesium glycinate: the sensible first move

If stress or a busy mind is what keeps you up, magnesium is the place to start, and glycinate is the form to use. Chronic stress can deplete magnesium, and the glycinate form is well absorbed and gentle on the gut, so it is unlikely to wake you with a bathroom trip the way cheaper forms can. Evidence tier: **Moderate**, and mechanism-led rather than dramatic. Our [magnesium for stress and sleep guide](/blog/magnesium-stress-sleep-guide/) explains why glycinate beats citrate and oxide for this use, and the [magnesium glycinate pick](/products/magnesium-glycinate/) lists what to check on a label. If you are considering pairing it with melatonin, read [can you take magnesium glycinate and melatonin together](/blog/can-you-take-magnesium-glycinate-and-melatonin-together/) first.

## L-theanine: to quiet a racing mind

L-theanine, an amino acid from tea, promotes a calm-but-alert state without sedation. For sleep that means it can help settle mental chatter at bedtime without the morning grogginess a sedative leaves behind. Evidence tier: **Moderate**. It is less about knocking you out and more about lowering the mental noise that keeps you awake. Our [L-theanine and calm focus review](/blog/l-theanine-calm-focus/) covers the research, and the [L-theanine pick](/products/l-theanine/) uses a standardized form.

## How to choose

Match the supplement to why you are awake. If stress and physical tension are the problem, start with **magnesium glycinate**. If it is a racing mind that will not switch off, **L-theanine** targets that more directly, and some people use both, magnesium nightly plus L-theanine on the worst nights. Neither replaces the basics that do the heavy lifting for sleep: a consistent schedule, a dark cool room, and cutting screens and caffeine before bed. Supplements are the small edge on top, not the foundation.

Add one at a time so you can tell what helps, and talk to a pharmacist or doctor before starting, especially if you take other medication.
