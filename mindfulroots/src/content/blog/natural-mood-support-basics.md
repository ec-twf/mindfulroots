This is a supplement site, so it might seem odd for us to say it — but the most effective things for everyday mood aren't on our shelf. They're free, they're unglamorous, and they're worth getting in place before you spend a cent on capsules.

## The foundations that actually move the needle

**Sleep.** Almost nothing affects mood as reliably as consistent, sufficient sleep. Fix this first.

**Daylight and movement.** Morning light helps regulate your body clock, and regular physical activity has some of the strongest mood evidence of anything — supplement or not.

**Food and connection.** A reasonably balanced diet covers most nutrient bases, and social connection is a powerful, under-rated buffer against low mood.

## Where supplements fit

Once the foundations are in place, supplements can play a small supporting role — correcting a likely deficiency (like [vitamin D](/products/vitamin-d3/) in winter), filling a dietary gap ([omega-3s](/products/omega-3-fish-oil/) if you don't eat oily fish), or supporting stress resilience ([magnesium](/products/magnesium-glycinate/) in the evening). Small role. Supporting. Not a substitute.

## And the most important line on this site

If low mood is interfering with your life, none of the above replaces talking to a doctor or therapist. Please see our [Get help](/get-help/) page — reaching out is the strongest move you can make.
