Saffron seems like an unlikely supplement, but standardized extracts have quietly built one of the more interesting mood-research records among botanicals.

## What the trials show

Several randomized, placebo-controlled studies using standardized saffron extracts report meaningful mood support in people with mild-to-moderate low mood — in some cases comparing favorably with reference treatments. The active compounds, crocin and safranal, appear to influence serotonin handling.

## Keep the population in mind

As with every supplement here, who was studied matters. Saffron's trials look at mild-to-moderate cases, often as a gentle option — not severe depression, and not as a replacement for professional care.

## Buying real saffron

Genuine saffron is one of the most expensive ingredients on earth, so a suspiciously cheap extract is a red flag. Look for a standardized extract (affron® is the most-studied) with stated crocin and safranal content, around the 28–30 mg/day used in trials.
