Most articles about 5-HTP lead with benefits. We're going to lead with the warning, because with this particular supplement that's the responsible order.

## The interaction that actually matters

5-HTP converts directly into serotonin in the body. That sounds appealing, but it's exactly why it can be dangerous alongside medications that *also* raise serotonin: SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, triptans for migraine, tramadol, and others. Stacking them risks **serotonin syndrome** — a potentially serious condition involving agitation, rapid heart rate, high temperature and more.

This is not a rare-edge-case warning. Many people interested in mood supplements are already taking, or considering, a prescription antidepressant — which is precisely the situation where 5-HTP can go wrong.

## So who is it for?

Honestly: only people who have checked with a doctor or pharmacist first, take no serotonergic medication, and use low doses without stacking other serotonin-active supplements. Pregnancy is a no.

If you're drawn to 5-HTP because you're struggling, please treat that as a signal to talk to a professional rather than to self-experiment. Our [Get help](/get-help/) page lists free, confidential options, and our [5-HTP profile](/products/5-htp/) repeats these cautions in full.
