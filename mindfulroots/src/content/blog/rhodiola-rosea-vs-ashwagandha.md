**Verdict first:** if your stress shows up mainly as *fatigue, low motivation, and mental tiredness*, rhodiola rosea is usually the better first try, because it acts quickly and is energising. If your stress shows up as *tension, racing thoughts, poor sleep, and a sense of being wired*, ashwagandha usually fits better; it is calming and sleep-supportive but slower to build. They are different plants with different chemistry, and matching the herb to your dominant symptom beats picking a universal "winner." ## Two adaptogens, two different jobs

Rhodiola rosea is an Arctic root whose active rosavins and salidroside are studied as support for fatigue and mental performance under stress. Ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*) is an Ayurvedic root whose withanolides are studied as support for perceived stress, cortisol, and sleep. "Adaptogen" groups them together, but their felt effects diverge: rhodiola tends to *lift*, ashwagandha tends to *settle*.

## What the evidence shows

For rhodiola, trials of the standardised SHR-5 extract reported reduced fatigue and improved mental performance in stressed or fatigued adults, including a study in physicians on night duty ([Olsson et al., 2009](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19016404/)). A later trial found rhodiola comparable to a low-dose antidepressant for mild depressive symptoms with fewer side effects, though the effect size was modest ([Mao et al., 2015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26502953/)).

For ashwagandha, the most-replicated protocol (300 mg of root extract twice daily) reduced perceived stress and cortisol over eight weeks ([Chandrasekhar et al., 2012](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23439798/)), and the independent [NCCIH overview](https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/ashwagandha) treats these stress and sleep findings as promising but still developing.

Both bodies of evidence are best described as *emerging to moderate*: consistent directions, small-to-medium trials, limited long-term data. Neither herb is a treatment for a diagnosed mood or anxiety disorder.

## What to look for in a rhodiola rosea supplement for stress and fatigue

If rhodiola matches your profile, useful selection criteria are:

- **Standardisation to ~3% rosavins and ~1% salidroside**, the roughly 3:1 ratio used in the trial-grade SHR-5 extract. A product that lists only "rhodiola 500 mg" without this ratio tells you little.
- **A studied dose**, generally 200-400 mg/day, taken in the morning.
- **Third-party testing**, since rhodiola is sometimes adulterated with cheaper look-alike species.
- **A single-species *Rhodiola rosea* label**, not a generic "rhodiola" blend.

## Cautions

Rhodiola can feel over-stimulating, may cause jitteriness or vivid dreams, and is best avoided late in the day. Ashwagandha can cause drowsiness or digestive upset, has been linked to rare liver injury, and can affect thyroid and blood-sugar levels. Both should be used cautiously in pregnancy, alongside medication, or with an existing condition. Talk with your clinician before starting either, and especially before combining them with prescription treatment.
