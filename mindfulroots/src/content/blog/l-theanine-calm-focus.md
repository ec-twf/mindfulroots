If you've ever noticed that green tea feels calmer than coffee despite both having caffeine, you've already met L-theanine. It's the closest thing the supplement world has to a "relaxed but alert" switch.

## What it actually does

L-theanine increases alpha brain-wave activity — the rhythm associated with calm, wakeful focus, the state you're in during quiet concentration. Crucially, it does this without sedation, so you don't trade anxiety for drowsiness.

## The caffeine partnership

This is L-theanine's party trick. Taken alongside caffeine, it smooths out the jitters and edginess while keeping the alertness. Many people who find coffee makes them anxious do fine with a caffeine + theanine combination.

## When to use it

Before a stressful meeting, during a wired afternoon, or in the evening when you want to downshift without feeling knocked out. It works within about an hour and has an excellent safety record.
