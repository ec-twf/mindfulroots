"Adaptogen" gets thrown around loosely, but two herbs have earned their place in the research literature: ashwagandha and rhodiola. The trick is that they pull in different directions — so picking the right one starts with naming your stress.

## Ashwagandha: when stress feels wired

Ashwagandha tends to *calm*. Randomized trials in stressed-but-healthy adults show reduced perceived stress and lower cortisol. It's the better fit if your stress shows up as racing thoughts, tension, or trouble winding down at night.

## Rhodiola: when stress feels like burnout

Rhodiola tends to *lift*. Its research centers on stress-related fatigue and mental performance under load. Choose it if your stress looks more like exhaustion, brain fog and running on empty — and take it in the morning, because it can be mildly stimulating.

## Both reward patience

Adaptogens aren't acute fixes. Give either a consistent 6–8 week trial before judging it, and remember the studied population is "stressed healthy adults" — not a clinical one.
