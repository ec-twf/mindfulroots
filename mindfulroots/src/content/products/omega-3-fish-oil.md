Omega-3 fatty acids are structural building blocks of brain tissue, and the body cannot make them efficiently on its own. Of the two main marine omega-3s, **EPA** is the one that mood-support research keeps coming back to: meta-analyses of randomized trials suggest formulas weighted toward EPA are more consistently associated with mood benefits than DHA-heavy ones.

It's important to keep the size of the effect in perspective. Omega-3s are a *supportive* nutrient — useful if your diet is low in oily fish, and reasonable to discuss with your doctor as a complement to professional care, but not a replacement for it.

## What to look for

- A clear EPA/DHA breakdown on the label (not just "fish oil 1000 mg")
- EPA-dominant ratios for mood-focused use
- Third-party testing (IFOS, USP, or similar) for purity and freshness
- Enteric-coated or triglyceride-form oils if fishy burps bother you
