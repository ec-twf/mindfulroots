The idea that bacteria in your gut can influence your mood sounds far-fetched until you learn how much chemical signalling travels along the vagus nerve, and how much serotonin is actually produced in the gut. Researchers now use the term *psychobiotics* for strains shown to affect stress and mood markers in trials.

This is genuinely early science — which is why we've rated it *emerging*. But it's a legitimate and well-tolerated area to explore, and it dovetails with the simple, unglamorous truth that looking after your gut tends to be good for the rest of you too.

## What to look for

- Named, research-backed strains rather than a giant CFU number alone
- Strains studied specifically for mood/stress, not just digestion
- Proper storage (some need refrigeration) and a guaranteed count through expiry
