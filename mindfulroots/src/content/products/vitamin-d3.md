Vitamin D behaves more like a hormone than a vitamin, and the brain is full of receptors for it. Observational studies repeatedly find that people with low vitamin D levels report lower mood — though trials show the clearest benefit when supplementation corrects an actual deficiency, rather than topping up someone who is already replete.

If you live at a northern latitude, work indoors, have darker skin, or simply spend most of the day inside, you are statistically quite likely to run low — which makes this one of the most rational "first checks" in any wellness routine.

## What to look for

- D3 (cholecalciferol) rather than D2 — it raises blood levels more reliably
- Softgels with oil, or take alongside a meal with fat for absorption
- Consider pairing with vitamin K2 if you're supplementing long-term
